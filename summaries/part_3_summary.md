# Part III Summary

## Title

**Conditions and Limits of Structural Redescription for Record-Deficient Cases**

---

## Core Proposition

Part III defines when structural redescription is permissible and when it must stop.

The paper addresses cases where original interaction records are incomplete, missing, or only partially available.

Its central claim is that record-deficient cases can sometimes be structurally redescribed, but only under strict limits.

---

## Role in the Series

Part III is the boundary-discipline layer of the AAS series.

Part I defines the framework.

Part II operationalizes AAS as a classifiable interaction state.

Part III prevents the framework from overreaching when evidence is incomplete.

Part IV / Extension builds on this by reconstructing a longitudinal case from a bounded archive.

---

## Key Concepts

### Structural Redescription

Structural redescription means describing the observable structure of an interaction from surviving artifacts.

It does not mean reconstructing every original event, intention, or causal pathway.

### Record-Deficient Cases

A record-deficient case is one in which the original interaction records are incomplete, unavailable, fragmented, or partially reconstructed.

Such cases require stricter interpretive discipline.

### Stopping Rules

Part III emphasizes that some claims must not be made when records are insufficient.

This is central to the integrity of the AAS framework.

---

## What This Paper Does

Part III defines the conditions under which a case may be redescribed from limited evidence.

It provides guardrails for distinguishing legitimate structural description from overinterpretation.

---

## What This Paper Does Not Claim

Part III does not claim that missing records can be fully recovered.

It does not claim that reconstruction can access hidden states.

It does not prove the original case in a strong causal sense.

It does not permit narrative completion where evidence is absent.

It does not convert partial records into total history.

---
