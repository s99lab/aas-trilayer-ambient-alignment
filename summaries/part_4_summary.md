# Part IV Summary

## Title

**Reconstructing Longitudinal Human-AI Interaction from a Bounded Archive: A Theory-Building Single-Case Process Analysis**

---

## Core Proposition

Part IV extends the AAS trilogy through bounded-archive reconstruction.

It asks how a longitudinal human-AI interaction can be analyzed when the available evidence consists of preserved outputs, summaries, documents, and bounded artifacts rather than a complete interaction history.

The paper treats the case neither as myth nor as invalid anecdote.

It treats it as a bounded process-analysis case.

---

## Role in the Series

Part IV is the bounded-archive extension of the AAS series.

It is related to the Core Trilogy, but it should not be read as merely a fourth installment.

It extends the trilogy by applying its descriptive and boundary-setting logic to a single longitudinal case.

---

## Key Concepts

### Bounded Archive

A bounded archive is a limited body of surviving records.

It may include outputs, documents, summaries, generated artifacts, or other preserved materials.

It does not contain the total interaction history.

### Single-Case Process Analysis

Part IV uses a single case to support theory-building and process description.

It does not claim statistical generalization.

### Reconstruction Without Overclaiming

The paper attempts to describe a longitudinal human-AI interaction process while avoiding claims that cannot be supported by the archive.

---

## What This Paper Does

Part IV reconstructs observable patterns from a bounded archive.

It examines how a sustained human-AI interaction can produce structured artifacts, role differentiation, and process continuity.

It uses the AAS framework while preserving the limitations established in Part III.

---

## What This Paper Does Not Claim

Part IV does not claim access to the full interaction history.

It does not claim AI consciousness or agency.

It does not claim that the case is statistically representative.

It does not claim that all causal pathways can be reconstructed.

It does not present the case as a mythic or exceptional proof of AI-human fusion.

---

## Relationship to Other Parts

Part I defines the AAS framework.

Part II operationalizes AAS as a classifiable interaction state.

Part III defines the limits of structural redescription.

Part IV applies those limits to bounded-archive process analysis.
